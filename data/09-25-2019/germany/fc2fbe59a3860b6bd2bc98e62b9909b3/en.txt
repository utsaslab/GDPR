ECJ ruling on social media plugins - website operators in the duty
On Monday, the European Court of Justice ruled on the issue of
Data protection compliant integration of a Facebook Like Button by website operators
(C-40/17). In a consistent continuation of his jurisprudence, he thus made common cause
Responsibility for Facebook Fanpages (C-210/16) states that it is also for the use of
So-called social plug-ins, such as the Facebook Like Button, a shared responsibility of
Operators of a homepage and the respective provider. Website operators can do that
Obtaining consent from users, insofar as such are required, as well as the
Do not postpone fulfillment of transparency obligations on Facebook.

The Berlin Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information recommends Berliners
When using social plugins, web site operators need to know exactly how far theirs
own responsibility is sufficient, which information obligations they fulfill towards the persons concerned
and on which legal basis the data processing should take place. If one
would ever fall back on such plugins, recommends the Berlin
Data Protection Officer for reasons of legal certainty, a consent-based solution
choose. Technically, care should be taken that personal data is not already
when calling the website.
