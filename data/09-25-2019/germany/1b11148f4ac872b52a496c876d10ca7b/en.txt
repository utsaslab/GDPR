Examination of an electronic health record by the
Berlin Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information

The Berlin Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information is currently examining one
Health insurance and private health insurance funded electronic health record
compliance with data protection and data security regulations. The product
allows patients to submit documents and data on their health and health
to collect medical treatment. Medical service providers may, upon request, include patient information in the electronic health record. Also health insurance
and insurance companies can use the file to target their insured,
their consent provided.
In the context of this audit, there were several deficiencies regarding the information of the
Insured, obtaining consent, ensuring data security and
the implementation of a privacy impact assessment. The provider receives now
Opportunity to comment on the identified shortcomings and eliminate them. In pieces
this has already happened.

Patient-related data should be encrypted by the healthcare provider prior to transmission in such a way that only the treated person himself can perform the decryption.
The service provider in this case has to ensure that the encryption only
with the key provided by the person being treated, and must
Prove the use of the correct key in case of doubt.
