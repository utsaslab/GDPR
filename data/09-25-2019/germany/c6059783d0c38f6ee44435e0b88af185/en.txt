PRESS RELEASE

3 June 2019

First IFG-Days Baden-Württemberg
Thursday, June 6, 2019: Cinema session with the country representative
As part of the first IFG-Days Baden-Württemberg presented the State Commissioner for
Data protection and freedom of information on Thursday, June 6, 2019 in cooperation with the
Stuttgart inner-city cinemas the thrilling intelligence thriller "The end of the truth".
Film beginning is at 20:00 clock in CINEMA, Königstr. 22 (2nd floor), Stuttgart.
A film by Philipp Leinemann with Axel Prahl, Claudia Michaelsen, Ronald Zehrfeld and
Alexander Fehling, the 2019 German Film Award, in the category: Best male
Minor role has received.
A film about intrigue, abuse of power and corruption.
To the movie:
Martin Behrens, an employee of the BND, passes on information that is a drone attack
the American result. A few days later there is an attack in one
Munich restaurant, in which Behrens secret lover comes to death.
Following the film, visitors can view the illuminated city silhouette
discuss their impressions together with the country representative at small nibbles.
The movie night will take place during the first IFG-Days Baden-Württemberg.
Further information about our IFG-Days can be found at:
https://www.baden-wuerttemberg.datenschutz.de/einladung-zu-den-ersten-ifg-days-in-bawue/
With the series of events entitled "Data Protection as a CULTURE Task", the LfDI examines the issue of data protection from different perspectives. This unusual approach finds
its continuation now in the field of freedom of information.
The country representative looks forward to an extraordinary evening with exciting and
stimulating conversations.
The entry fee of 7.80 euros includes admission to the cinema, nibbles after the
Film in the foyer and an incomparable view of the Schlossplatz.
Tickets are available now at the box office of the downtown cinemas and at:
