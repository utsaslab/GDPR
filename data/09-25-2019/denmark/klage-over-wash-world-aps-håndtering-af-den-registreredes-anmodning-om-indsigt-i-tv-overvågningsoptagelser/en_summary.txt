1, the data subject has the right to obtain the data controller's confirmation as to whether personal data concerning the data subject is being processed and, where appropriate, access to the personal data and the following information:


the purposes of the treatment


the categories of personal data concerned


the recipients or categories of recipients to whom personal data is or will be disclosed, in particular recipients in third countries or international organizations;


if possible, the intended period during which personal data will be stored or, if this is not possible, the criteria used to determine that period;


the right to request the data controller to correct or delete personal data or to limit the processing of personal data concerning the data subject or to object to such processing


the right to file a complaint with a supervisory authority


any information available from where the personal data originates, if not collected from the data subject


the existence of automatic decisions, including profiling, as referred to in Article 22 (2); 1 and 4, and at least meaningful information about the logic therein, as well as the significance and expected consequences of such processing for the data subject. In the decision, the Data Protection Authority noted that if the data subject requests access to television surveillance footage, which includes information about other persons, the data controller must blur or otherwise remove them by, for example, using image cropping.
