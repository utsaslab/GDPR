In the context of electoral activities and political campaigns, respect for data protection rules is key to protecting democracy. At the same time, it is also a means of maintaining citizens&#39; confidence and the integrity of elections. Political parties, political coalitions and candidates are increasingly relying on personal information and complex profiling techniques to monitor voters and opinion makers and target them. The proliferation of such data processing techniques for political purposes poses serious risks - not only for the right to privacy and data protection, but also for confidence in the integrity of the democratic process. That is why the European Data Protection Board (ECSC) has adopted a statement on the use of personal data in political campaigns, highlighting a number of key points that must be addressed in connection with elections to the European Parliament and other EU elections scheduled for 2019. respect political parties in the processing of personal data during election activities.
